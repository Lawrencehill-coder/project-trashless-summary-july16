I.

Executive Summary

Agvisors has an LOI in place to acquire the 608-acre former Rio Grande Valley Sugar Growers mill site in Santa Rosa, Texas, with the intent of converting the existing industrial infrastructure into Project Trashless — a "never-fill-the-hole" recycling and waste-to-value campus processing asphalt shingles, tires, and plastics, layered with a senior-oriented RV/cottage-home resort development on the site's agricultural acreage.

The sponsor is currently negotiating a $7M acquisition price against a co-op seller (down from a rejected $8.6M offer roughly 18 months ago), backed in part by an offshore investor who has committed $8M toward acquisition financing. The sponsor's broader ask is $65M in equity or blended capital to fund site development, offering up to 40% equity plus stated tax advantages and optional board participation. I was approached to evaluate a role for Pedison USA in the deal — most likely in a Phase II development-financing and/or corporate branding capacity, following the site acquisition. I'm bringing this to you and the committee now for a read on fit and next steps.

VII.

Texas Market Context

Sponsor characterizes the South Texas / Rio Grande Valley market as relatively uncrowded for this concept — existing commercial recycled-shingle/asphalt plants are concentrated further north in the state, citing a "McKinney operation" that reportedly processes ~90-98% of the used asphalt shingles it takes in in Texas into road material and new shingles named operator not independently confirmed. Publicly traded comparables in adjacent categories include Arcosa, CRH, Waste Management, Owens Corning, and GAF. TxDOT recognizes recycled asphalt shingles (RAS) and reclaimed asphalt pavement (RAP) as approved inputs, with a stated goal (per sponsor) of roughly 10–15% recycled shingle content in state asphalt mixes — a real, if modest, downstream demand driver.

I noted I'm not unfamiliar with the underlying tooling here — I've seen plastics and tire-shredding operations firsthand, and I'm aware of a small pyrolysis operation (running since ~2017-18) that processes medical/biohazard waste — so I wasn't starting the conversation cold on the technical side. I let Robert know that Phase II — financing the build-out — sits squarely within Pedison's wheelhouse, and that we'd be able to perform if engaged. My preference, though, is to sit down with all the principals on our next discovery call to determine whether it would be appropriate for Pedison to join on the acquisition side as well, and to lead with Branding first so capital gets raised the correct and proper way. Pedison will explore funding the entire deal. I stressed upfront that my recommendation is to start with Branding, since that's what makes every other phase work properly.

Recommendation — sequencing before offshore capital commits Robert is currently planning to rely on the Philippines-based investor to source the initial $8M to acquire the facility (Anthony Smith sits in on these calls but is not a participant in the deal itself). I raised directly on the call that offshore capital rules have changed substantially, and that Robert has confirmed he has not yet registered an S-1 (or equivalent offering document) for this project. Given that, I proposed we hold a full, thorough second discovery call with all principals present — myself, Robert, and his partner — before Robert commits to using the offshore investor's money, rather than after. I think this sequencing matters: getting the offering structure and source-of-funds picture right up front avoids having to unwind a misstep later.
Recommendation — branding as the first engagement I saw an opening to pitch Robert directly on Pedison's Corporate Branding service — we tell the story and raise capital around it — and I intend to encourage Branding as the first step in any engagement with this sponsor, consistent with our redefined Three-Service Doctrine (Branding, Structuring, and Capital Raise held as independent, sequenced services). This also aligns with how we're steering our capital-raise pipeline more deliberately toward Allion Fund I mandates, which require a real estate component — this project's 608-acre site and mill structure clears that bar.

I'd like the committee's read on both points above — the proposed second discovery call before any offshore capital commits, and leading with Branding as our first engagement with this sponsor.
